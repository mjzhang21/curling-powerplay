      \begin{block}{Introduction}
        \heading{Power Play and Game-State Strategy}

        Mixed doubles curling allows the \textbf{Power Play} once per match for the
        hammer team. Using shot-level data and statistical models, we quantify how
        Power Play usage affects \textbf{offensive shot selection} and \textbf{scoring
          outcomes} across different game situations.

        \begin{itemize}
          \item How does Power Play use affect shot selection and scoring depending on score
                state and end group?
          \item When does the Power Play provide a clear strategic advantage, and what are the
                associated risk–reward trade-offs?
        \end{itemize}
      \end{block}

      \begin{block}{Data and Methods}
        \heading{End-level outcomes and tactical models summarize gameplay}

        Shot-by-shot data from mixed doubles curling matches were analyzed to measure
        hammer-team performance. Only complete ends were included. Hammer-team shots
        were classified as \textbf{offensive} (aimed at scoring) or \textbf{defensive}
        (aimed at limiting opponent scoring).

        Predicted probabilities were estimated using binomial logistic regression
        (offensive shot probability) and multinomial logistic regression (end outcomes:
        0 / 1 / 2+ points), controlling for score state, end group, Power Play, and
        team strength. Only statistically significant coefficients are interpreted when
        describing effects.

        \small
        \begin{center}
          \textbf{Key Variables}
          \begin{tabular}{ll}
            \toprule
            Variable        & Description                           \\
            \midrule
            Power Play      & 1 = used, 0 = not used                \\
            Score state     & Leading / Tied / Trailing             \\
            End group       & Early (≤6) / Late (≥7)                \\
            Offensive shots & Aggressive scoring shots              \\
            Defensive shots & Protective / takeout shots            \\
            Outcome         & Points scored: 0 / 1 / 2+             \\
            Strength diff   & Standardized team strength difference \\
            \bottomrule
          \end{tabular}
        \end{center}

      \end{block}

      \begin{block}{Discussion}
        \heading{Power Play Effects}

        \begin{itemize}
          \item Power Play increases offensive shots and scoring opportunities.
          \item Largest benefits occur for teams trailing late in the game.
          \item Minimal gains for leading teams; slight reductions in variability in some
                cases.
          \item Best used strategically to recover points rather than extend a lead.
          \item Observational data; future work could explore team styles and opponent
                strategies.
        \end{itemize}
        \vspace{1em}
      \end{block}

      \begin{alertblock}{Key Takeaway}
        \textbf{Power Play is most effective when trailing late, boosting offensive shots and multi-point scoring with little downside when used strategically.}
      \end{alertblock}
