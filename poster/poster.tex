    \begin{column}{0.2\paperwidth}
      \heading{Introduction}
      \begin{block}{Power Play creates a high-risk, high-reward scoring opportunity}
        \begin{itemize}
          \item Mixed doubles curling allows a \textbf{Power Play} once per match for the
                hammer team.
          \item Repositions initial stones to create an \textbf{aggressive scoring
                  opportunity}.
          \item Key question: How does Power Play use affect \textbf{shot selection} and
                \textbf{scoring outcomes} across game situations?
        \end{itemize}
      \end{block}

      \heading{Data and Methods}
      \begin{block}{End-level outcomes and tactical models summarize gameplay}
        Data consist of shot-by-shot records from elite mixed doubles curling matches.
        \cite{ritchie2025opening}

        We construct end-level outcomes (0, 1, 2+ points) and estimate:
        \begin{itemize}
          \item Shot selection models (offensive vs defensive shots)
          \item Multinomial logit models of scoring outcomes
        \end{itemize}

        All models control for score state, end timing, and team strength.
      \end{block}
    \end{column}

        Power plays are used selectively rather than uniformly across game states
        (Figure 1). When deployed, teams adopt a more \textbf{offensive shot
          selection}, particularly when trailing (Figure 2). This strategic shift
        increases the probability of scoring multiple points but also raises the
        likelihood of scoring zero (Figure 3). Consistent with this tradeoff, Power
        Plays increase the \textbf{variability of scoring outcomes}, indicating a
        higher-risk, higher-reward strategy (Figure 4).
      \end{block}
    \end{column}

    \separatorcolumn

    \begin{column}{0.2\paperwidth}
      \heading{Discussion}
      \begin{block}{Power Play provides context-dependent strategic advantage}

        The results suggest that the Power Play is a \textbf{high-risk, high-reward
          strategy}. Teams use it in specific situations and adjust their shot selection
        accordingly. While it increases the chance of scoring multiple points, it also
        raises the likelihood of scoring zero, leading to greater outcome variability.
        These findings highlight a strategic tradeoff between \textbf{consistency} and
        \textbf{upside}, with implications for optimal decision-making in competitive
        play.

      \end{block}
